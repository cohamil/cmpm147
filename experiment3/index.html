Experiment 3 - Alternate Worlds

Codes:

  • underscore: "_" background
  • period: "." room ground
  • line: "|" wall
  • tilda: "~" water
  • hash: "#" chest

(using tiles from Micro Tileset - Overworld & Dungeon by thkaspar)

Codes:

  • underscore: "_" dark grass
  • period: "." light grass
  • line: "|" dirt
  • tilda: "~" water
  • carrot: "^" flower

(using tiles from Micro Tileset - Overworld & Dungeon by thkaspar)

Description

With this project, I was tasked with creating a Dungeon generator and editor, with random room creation and water/chest spawning. Additionally, an Overworld generator and editor, with random biome generation. Enter codes into the ASCII representation to change the rendering of the overworld.

Technical

Using the tileset linked above, I created a generative dungeon/biome generator according to a graphed ASCII representation of the rendered image. Once the page loads, the biome/dungeon is generated, then rendered many times a second, allowing the user to change the ASCII represenation. With this functionality, an editor is provided to directly enter the codes listed and change the render.

Reflection

This was a great project for getting me to think about how procedural generation can be used to create landscapes. One of the most difficult parts of this project was creating the overall style of both the dungeon and overworld, as I am not an artist and had to try out a bunch of different variations of the tiles provided in the tileset. Ultimately, I feel that this project is successful in bringing life and random generation to both the rendered images of dungeon and overworld.
